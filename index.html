About

The Person Behind the Code

I am a Computer Science Educator and Software Developer with a grounding in Industrial Mathematics and Applied Statistics. My professional path has taken me from the classroom to operations management, from data analytics to full-stack development — and back to teaching again, this time bringing real-world engineering experience into the classroom.

Currently based in Gombi, Adamawa State, I work at St. Stephen of Hungary College where I teach CS and oversee the development of institutional software systems. I believe deeply that technology built for Nigerian institutions must account for real infrastructure constraints — and I design accordingly.

My development philosophy centres on pragmatic, maintainable architecture. I favour Flask for backend systems, lean heavily on HTMX and Alpine.js for progressive enhancement, and invest in clear database design before writing a single route. Whether I am building a school management portal, a multi-tenant POS sync engine, or a local AI assistant, I apply the same discipline: understand the problem fully, then engineer deliberately.

Beyond code, I am a statistician and data analyst comfortable with Bayesian probability, correlation analysis, and error modelling — skills I apply in professional evaluations, system design, and undergraduate-level tutoring.

I am not interested in surface-level conversation. I am drawn to fact-grounded, critical discussions about systems — why they are the way they are, where they break, and how to make them genuinely better. I rarely rush to judgement; I prefer to go behind the scene and understand the root of a thing before I act. That patience is also what makes me decisive when speed is needed — accumulated understanding makes the right path obvious.

I follow defined patterns deliberately and only deviate when I can clearly articulate why change is warranted. I explore and test new things freely — I flow like water and refine like fire. Freedom and truth are the two things I will not compromise on.
